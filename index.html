Research

My research has spread throughout different domains in robotics. Most of my work so far has related to multi-object grasping and robotic cloud computing. My most recent project is related to learning locomotion policies on a Quadruped. I have always been interested in using computer vision to leverage navigation/locomotion techniques on robotic systems. I want robots to learn how they can use their environment and control their body to traverse through any environment.

Push-MOG: Efficient Pushing to Consolidate Polygonal Objects for Multi-Object Grasping
Shrey Aeron, Edith Llontop, Aviv Adler, Wisdom C. Agboh, Mehmet Dogar, and Ken Goldberg
Accepted at CASE 2023

We present Push-MOG, an algorithm that creates efficient object clusters, plans stable pushes to rearrange objects within those clusters, and generates multi-object grasps for the rearranged object clusters.
